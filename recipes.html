Dosa

Ingredients
Urad dal: 1 cup
Rice:3 cup
Cooked Rice: ½ cup
Fenugreek: ½ tsp
Water: according to need
Salt: as per need
Oil: as per need

Recipe

Soak urad dal, rice, and fenugreek for 5-6 hours. And grind it along with the cooked rice and water until it reaches a smooth consistency. Then keep it aside for 6-7 hours for fermentation. The batter is ready to use. You can add some oil in the batter for more crispiness. Heat a flat surface iron pan or non-stick pan, then (apply oil for iron pan) pour ½ a cup of dosa batter into it and spread it into a round shape. When one side is cooked, flip it to the other side and cook it until it turns golden brown color. Dosa is ready to serve. Continue the process for rest of the batter.
**The batter can be stored in a refridgerator for 2 days**

Calories=150 per dosa

Pancake

Ingredients
All-purpose Flour: 1 cup
Sugar: 2 tbs
Baking Powder: 2 tsp
Salt: ½ tsp
Milk: 1 cup
Melted Butter: 2 tbs
Vanilla Extract: 1 tsp
Egg: 1

Recipe

In a bowl, whisk together milk, butter, vanilla extract and egg. Make it soft and fluffy. Add the dry ingredients to it and mix it. Don’t overmix. Preheat your non stick pan or griddle in a low flame. About 2 mins. Lightly grease your pan with a little butter. Take a ¼ cup of batter (to get evenly shaped pancakes). Start pouring from the middle, then continue pouring slowly in a circular motion so that the batter spreads into perfect round shapes. Don’t rush the cooking by over heating or increased flame. In a meantime, you will see bubbles starting to form on top (batter side). Now flip your pancake, using a spatula. (use your wrist instead of your whole hands for flipping). Cook the other side as well. Your easy spongy pancakes are ready to be served.
**should be freshly eaten**

Calories=227 per pancake

Oats Putt

Ingredients
Oats: 1 cup
Salt: as per need
Grinded Coconut: ½ cup
Water: as per need

Recipe

Mix ¼ cup of warm water, oats and salt in a container. After mixing well, sprinkle some water all over, mix it. Keep sprinkling water and mix it until it gets into a crumbled form. In a vessel boil 2 cups of water and pass this vapour into this dough for 5 mins or more. (Specially made puttu vessels are available through which vapour can be passed) Puttu is ready.
**This cannot be stored for so long, cosume it hot**

Calories=200 per serving

Oats meal

Ingredients
Old- Fashion Oats: ½ cup
Milk: ½ cup
Water: ½ cup
Salt: a pinch
Banana: 1 sliced
Berries/ fruits: topping
Maple syrup: topping

Recipe

In a microwave bowl add oats, salt, water and milk. (oats to liquid ratio, making it 1:2 gives a perfect consistency and milk or water can be taken in any ratio, here I have taken in 1:1 ratio). Heat the mixture for 2 minutes in microwave. Now the base is ready to serve. Add sliced bananas, nuts, maple syrup for toppings. Or fruits for a complete healthy way. Be creative with the toppings. Enjoy!
**should be freshly eaten**

Calories=158 per cup

Kesari

Ingredients
Semolina: 1 cup
Granule Sugar: ¾ Cup
Ghee: ½ - 1 cup
Food colour: yellow 1 tsp
Warm Water: 2 cup
Cardamom: 3-4
Cashew Nuts / saffron: For decorations

Recipe

Heat a pan, pour ghee into it. Fry cashew nuts in it and keep it aside. In the same pan, roast the semolina as well (until it has a good fragrance). Mix the yellow food color with warm water. And pour this water into the semolina in intervals. Keep stirring it, such that there won’t be any lumps formed. Add sugar simultaneously along with adding water. Make sure the mixture is combined well. When the semolina thickens, add crushed cardamom to it. Cover and simmer this mixture for 2 mins till it cooks well. Add more ghee and sort well until the Kesari separates from the pan. Now Kesari is ready to serve. Add Roasted cashews and saffron on top of it for decoration.
**store kesari in an air-tight conatiner and refrigerate.**

Calories=189 per cup

Mug Cake

Ingredients
All-purpose flour:1/4 cup + 1 ½ tsp
Powdered Sugar: 2 tbs
Baking Powder: ¼ tsp
Salt: 1 pinch
Milk : 3 tbs
Vanilla Essence : ½ tsp
Sprinklers: if required

Recipe

Add all the dry ingredients into a mug and mix well. Into that, add the liquid ingredients and make it a smooth blend. Add the sprinklers if required. Cook it in the microwave for 2 mins and leave it there for 1 min before consuming. (take it out when it becomes shiny outside). Here, it is decorated with Nutella, as I personally like Nutella so much. You can add whichever toppings according to your choice.
**can be stored in refridgerator for upto 3 days**

Calories=200 per cup

Chocolate Milk

Ingredients
Dark chocolate: 1 tbs (shredded)
Cocoa Powder: 1 ½ tbs
Sugar: 1 tbs (more can be added according to your choice)
Milk: 1 cup (hot/cold)
Water: ¼ cup

Recipe

Ingredients In a milk pan, boil water. Simultaneously double boil dark chocolate. When the water boils, add cocoa powder, sugar (now or can be added later, when serving), melted dark chocolate and heat it form a thick mixture. Allow it to cool down. Add milk now and blend it very well. Now your chocolate milk is ready to serve. Add chocolate syrup if needed.
** the mix prepared can be stored in air tight container in refridgerator for almost 1 week**

Calories=130 per glass

Butter milk

Ingredients
Thick curd: 1 cup
Salt: as per need
Cold Water:2 cup
Ginger: ½ tbs
Green Chilli: 1
Curry leaves: ¼ cup (crushed)

Recipe

Blend curd, salt and 1 cup of water without any lumps. And chopped ginger and green chilli to this and mix well. Add 1 more cup of water to it as well as the curry leaves. Mix it well. And serve it chill.
**consume freshly**

Calories=50 per glass
